# notes/ — your study journal + tracking

This is where you track your day-by-day progress through [`STUDY-PLAN.md`](../STUDY-PLAN.md) and capture your own learning.

## What goes where

| File | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `STUDY-PLAN.md` (in repo root) | **Day-by-day checklist**. Tick `[ ]` → `[x]` as you finish each day. The "Progress checklist" section at the top is the canonical "where am I" view. |
| `notes/YYYY-MM-DD.md` | **Daily recap**. One per study day. Use `_daily-template.md` as the starting point. |
| `notes/week-N-checkin.md` | **Weekly self-assessment** every Sunday. Use `_weekly-checkin-template.md`. |
| `notes/weak-spots.md` | **Append-only weak-spots list**. Add to it whenever you fumble. Drill these between study days. |

## The daily workflow

**Morning** (5 min):
1. Open `STUDY-PLAN.md`, see today's day. Click into the listed simple docs.
2. Copy `_daily-template.md` → `notes/YYYY-MM-DD.md`. Fill in the topic line at the top.

**During the session** (1-1.5 h):
3. Read the simple companions for the day.
4. Answer each killer question **out loud**. Mark ✅/❌ in today's note.
5. Add `❌` topics to `weak-spots.md` if they feel like a real gap (not just first-encounter haze).

**End of session** (5-10 min):
6. Fill in "What clicked" + "What's still fuzzy" in today's note.
7. Tick the day's `[ ]` → `[x]` in `STUDY-PLAN.md`'s Progress checklist.
8. Set "Tomorrow's prep" line if needed.

## The weekly workflow

**Sunday end of week** (15 min):
1. Copy `_weekly-checkin-template.md` → `notes/week-N-checkin.md`
2. Honest self-assessment — answer the 5 prompts
3. Count: how many days did I complete this week? Am I on pace?
4. Decide: any adjustment for next week?

## File naming convention

- Daily recaps: `YYYY-MM-DD.md` (e.g., `2026-06-06.md`)
- Weekly check-ins: `week-N-checkin.md` (e.g., `week-1-checkin.md`)
- Templates start with `_` so they sort to the top and are clearly templates

## Why this works

- **Active recall** (out-loud drilling + writing in your own words) is 10× more effective than passive re-reading.
- **Spaced revisit** via `weak-spots.md` ensures you don't skim past gaps.
- **Weekly self-assessment** catches "I'm-actually-falling-behind" early enough to course-correct.
- **Visible progress** (the ticked checkboxes) is a motivation engine — you can *see* week 1 of 6 closing out.

## What NOT to do

- Don't copy-paste from the docs into your notes. The whole value is **rewriting in your own words**.
- Don't skip the out-loud drill. Reading the killer question silently is not interview practice.
- Don't try to catch up on missed days by doing 2x the work. Just slip forward 1 day. The plan has built-in light days for this.
- Don't pad your `weak-spots.md` with everything. Add real gaps, not just "I'd like to re-read this."
